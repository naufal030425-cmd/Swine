Swine Mass Accretion & Derivative Dynamics

Tracking instantaneous lean-tissue velocity curves to mathematically dictate optimal finisher marketplace allocation.

MODEL: Landrace x Large White Crossbreed (Standard Farrow-to-Finish Matrix)

■ Biological Problem Matrix

In precision swine husbandry, feed constitutes over 60–70% of total production costs. Swine growth curves follow a sigmoidal pattern where muscle accumulation scales fast during early phases before transitioning heavily toward backfat layer deposition.

By deploying a cubic biological function $f(x)$ mapping body weight against weeks, managers can solve for the first derivative $f'(x)$. This yields the **instantaneous rate of change**, providing exact data on when the animal reaches maximum growth velocity before feed conversion ratios drop.

Calculus Kernel

CUMULATIVE WEIGHT FUNCTION $f(x)$
$f(x) = -0.011x^3 + 0.48x^2 + 1.2x + 1.5$
GROWTH VELOCITY DERIVATIVE $f'(x)$
$f'(x) = -0.033x^2 + 0.96x + 1.2$
• $x$: Finisher Age Timeline (Weeks 0 to 24)
• $f(x)$: Cumulative Animal Mass (kg)
• $f'(x)$: Instantaneous Weight Gain Velocity (kg/week)
Telemetry Calibration Source: Growth constants adjusted against historical commercial production standards found in the PIC (Pig Improvement Company) Nutrient Requirements & Growth Reference Charts.
